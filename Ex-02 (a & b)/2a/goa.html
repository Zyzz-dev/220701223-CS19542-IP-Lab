Goa

Goa, located on India’s southwestern coast, is a captivating state known for its unique blend of culture, beaches, and vibrant nightlife. Here are some key highlights:

  • Geography: Goa comprises both a mainland district and an offshore island. It lies about 250 miles (400 km) south of Mumbai (Bombay) and is bordered by Maharashtra to the north and Karnataka to the east and south. The Arabian Sea graces its western coastline.
  • Economy: Goa boasts a state domestic product estimated at $11 billion (2017), making it India’s richest state. Its GDP per capita is two and a half times that of the country, with a remarkable growth rate of 8.23% (yearly average from 1990 to 2000)
  • Attractions: Explore endless sandy beaches, indulge in eclectic seafood, and witness world-heritage listed architecture. Don’t miss the beautiful Basilica of Bom Jesus, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing exquisite baroque architecture
